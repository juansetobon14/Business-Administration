Business Administration

What is it?

Business administration, according to the College of Central Florida, is "a program that trains individuals to assume management or supervisory roles in industry, business, and government." What this means is that it trains people to organize employees and finances of a company.

Why Business Administration?

This major has a lot of opportunities, not only because you can get into different careers but also in different fields, giving you the opportunity to experiment and grow.

Different Functional Areas

There are five functional areas in business administration that help a business operate smoothly and maintain organization:

  • Marketing
  • Operations
  • Finance
  • Human Resources
  • Research and Development

Definition of the Five Areas

  • Marketing: Relates to the product's consumption, observes customers to create demand.
  • Operations: Supervises the production process and manages inventory.
  • Finance: Manages the business's money, the investments, and how much people are getting paid.
  • Human Resources: Manages everything related to employees, wages, recruitment, and compensation.
  • Research and Development: Innovates and creates new strategies to keep a competitive edge.

Structure

To become a professional in business administration, consider the time and money you want to invest, the benefits you would get, and the kind of job you are looking for.

Career Paths

As mentioned before, there are five different paths a business student can take. These paths depend on the abilities and aptitudes of each person.
